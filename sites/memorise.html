The Birth of Memorise:

A Story of Shared Adventures begins (in the wildest wildnes of Sweden)

The inspiration for Memorise struck during an unforgettable backpacking trip through Sweden's Kungsleden trail. This wasn't just any vacation - it was a journey amongst friends, a chance to forge memories that would last a lifetime. Cameras clicked constantly, capturing breathtaking landscapes, hilarious mishaps, and moments of pure camaraderie.


But as the excitement of the trip faded, a familiar challenge emerged: sharing the captured memories. Emails flooded inboxes, overflowing with photos and snippets of the adventure. The sheer volume made it impossible to compile a cohesive narrative, and precious moments risked being lost in the digital clutter.




This struggle wasn't new. Every group trip, every milestone event, seemed to follow the same pattern. Photos scattered across devices, stories half-told, the magic of shared experiences slowly fading. There had to be a better way.


That's when the idea for Memorise blossomed. We envisioned a platform that could centralize everything related to a specific memory - a digital campfire for friends to gather around and relive their experiences. No more lost photos, no more fragmented stories. Just a shared space to collaboratively build a rich tapestry of memories.


Memorise was born from a desire to bridge the gap between captured moments and lasting memories. With the app, you can easily create shared "memory capsules" for any event - a trip with the guys, a birthday celebration, a family reunion. Invite your friends, add photos, stories, even locations, and together, co-curate a digital record of your shared adventure.


The story of Memorise isn't just about technology - it's about the power of shared experiences and the enduring bonds forged through them. We hope that Memorise becomes your go-to tool for preserving those precious moments, ensuring that the stories you create together live on, just as vibrant as the day they were made.

The Kungsleden (King's Trail)

The Kungsleden, translating to "King's Trail" in English, is a renowned 440-kilometer (273-mile) hiking route located in Swedish Lapland. It traverses some of the country's most remote and scenic landscapes, passing through vast wilderness areas and skirting the highest mountains in Sweden. The trail is known for its breathtaking arctic vistas, including glaciers, alpine valleys, and pristine lakes.

While considered a moderate hike in terms of technical difficulty, the Kungsleden presents several challenges for hikers. Here's a breakdown of some key factors:
  • Remoteness: The trail traverses sparsely populated regions, with limited access to amenities and support services. Hikers need to be self-sufficient, carrying essential supplies and equipment for the duration of their trek.
  • Distance: Completing the entire Kungsleden is a significant undertaking, requiring a well-conditioned physique and the ability to cover long distances on foot. Hikers often choose to section-hike the trail over several trips.
  • Weather: The climate in Swedish Lapland can be harsh, with unpredictable weather conditions. Hikers need to be prepared for a range of possibilities, including rain, wind, snow, and cold temperatures, even during the summer months.

Despite the challenges, the Kungsleden offers a unique and rewarding experience for hikers seeking an adventure in a breathtaking natural setting.

The Technology Behind Memorise


Building Blocks

  • Node.js (API): A powerful JavaScript runtime environment that forms the foundation of Memorise's backend. It allows us to create a scalable and efficient API for handling data requests.
  • MySQL (Database): A robust and reliable relational database that securely stores all your precious memories and their associated information.

Frontend & User Experience

  • Angular with TypeScript (JavaScript Framework): A dynamic JavaScript framework that structures the user interface and application logic. It provides a clean and maintainable codebase for a smooth user experience.
  • HTML5: The core markup language that defines the structure and content of the web pages within the app.
  • SCSS: A powerful extension of CSS that simplifies styling with variables, mixins, and nesting, allowing for a clean and maintainable stylesheet.
  • Angular Material: A pre-built library of UI components that streamline the development process and provide a consistent, beautiful user interface.

Scalability & Security

  • Firebase Authentication: A secure and user-friendly authentication service that handles user login and account management.
  • Firebase Functions: A serverless framework that allows us to deploy lightweight backend functionality without managing servers.
  • Google Cloud Storage: A scalable and cost-effective storage solution for efficiently storing all your uploaded photos and media.

Additional Features

  • Google Maps API: Integrates interactive maps into the app, allowing you to visualize the locations associated with your memories.
  • Google Cloud SQL: A managed relational database service on Google Cloud Platform, offering a robust and scalable solution for data storage.
